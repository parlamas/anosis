ADVERBIAL CLAUSES
  1. Adverbial clauses of place

    Adverbial clauses of place describe where the action in a sentence’s main clause takes place. See how they work in these examples:

    1. My son told me another fight broke out where he eats lunch at school. (place)
      Min søn fortalte mig, at et andet slagsmål brød ud, hvor han spiser frokost i skolen. (bryde-brød-brudt)

    2. They drove beyond where the city ends. (place)
      De kørte ud over, hvor byen ender.

  2. Adverbial clauses of time

    Adverbial clauses of time communicate when the action in a sentence takes place:

    1. Before she got home, she called and ordered a pizza. (time)
      Inden hun kom hjem, ringede hun og bestilte en pizza.

    2. The team assembled, dressed, and marched out as the band were playing. (time)
      Holdet samledes, klædte sig på og marcherede ud, mens bandet spillede.

  3. Adverbial clauses of condition

    With an adverbial clause of condition, you can communicate the conditions related to the verb, adverb, or adjective in the sentence’s main clause.

    These examples demonstrate a few ways to use adverbial clauses of condition:

    1. We’ll be sitting in the conference room until they accept our terms. (condition)
      Vi vil sidde i mødelokalet, indtil de accepterer vores vilkår.

    2. Whether my husband likes it or not, we’ll be celebrating Christmas at my parents’ house. (condition)
      Uanset om min mand kan lide det eller ej, vil vi fejre jul hos mine forældre.

  4. Adverbial clauses of reason

    Adverbial clauses of reason tell us the reason for the action being taken in the sentence’s main clause. These clauses generally use subordinating conjunctions like because, unless, and since.

    Here are a few examples of sentences that include adverbial clauses of reason:

    1. We adopted these two cats because we love them. (reason)
      Vi adopterede disse to børn, fordi vi elsker dem.

    2. They woke up late, so they missed their flight. (result)
      De vågnede sent, så de missede deres fly.

    3. He’s amazing at dribbling since he spent his youth playing for Barcelona. (reason)
      Han er fantastisk til at drible, siden han brugte sin ungdom på at spille for Barcelona.

  5. Adverbial clauses of purpose

    Like adverbial clauses of reason, adverbial clauses of purpose frequently involve subordinating conjunctions. These two kinds of clauses can look similar, but they have one key difference: While adverbial clauses of reason give the reason why something is happening, adverbial clauses of purpose explain the reason to take a specific action.

    Here are a few examples:

    1. We studied all night so we would pass the exam. (purpose)
      Vi studerede hele natten, så vi ville bestå eksamen.

    2. So that they could ease the traffic flow, the event organizers split the group into three cohorts. (purpose)
      For at de kunne lette trafikken, delte arrangørerne gruppen op i tre kohorter.

  6. Adverbial clauses of comparison

    Adverbial clauses of comparison are clauses that communicate how the subject of the dependent clause compares to the subject in the main clause.

    There are two types of adverbial clauses of comparison: adverbial clauses of comparison of degree and adverbial clauses of comparison of manner.

    Here are a few examples of adverbial clauses of comparison or degree:

    1. Theodore is as good at video games as he is good at weight lifting. (comparison of degree)
      Theodore er lige så god til videospil, som han er god til vægtløftning.

    2. We expected the afternoon class to perform better on the test than they did last year. (comparison of degree)
      Vi forventede, at eftermiddagsklassen ville klare sig bedre på testen, end de gjorde sidste år.

    Here are a few examples of adverbial clauses of comparison of manner:

    1. The events unfolded as the oracle prophesized. (comparison of manner)
      Begivenhederne udspillede sig, som oraklet profeterede.

    2. The invasion did not play out as the dictator had hoped. (comparison of manner)
      Invasionen udspillede sig ikke, som diktatoren havde håbet.

  7. Adverbial clause of concession

    In an adverbial clause of concession, the writer acknowledges or admits a factor that modifies the main clause.

    Take a look at these adverbial clauses of concession:

    1. Although they did their best, they lost the game. (concession)
      Selvom de gjorde deres bedste, tabte de kampen.

    2. The department head hired the first person they interviewed, despite the fact that twenty people applied for the job. (concession)
      Afdelingslederen ansatte den første, de interviewede, på trods af at tyve personer søgte jobbet.
More examples of adverbial clauses

Previously, we demonstrated how adverbial clauses can appear at the beginning or at the end of a sentence. They can also appear in the middle, as they do in these examples:

  1. Our team, whenever they win a match, act like a swarm of bees and comes together as a cohesive unit.
    Vores hold, når de vinder en kamp, fungerer som en bisværm og samles som en sammenhængende enhed.

  2. Jill sat, because her usual spot at the table was taken, in a chair in the corner.
    Jill sad, fordi hendes sædvanlige plads ved bordet blev taget, i en stol i hjørnet.

Adverbial clauses can be long, sometimes even longer than the main clause:

  1. Because there was ice on the road and I had already slipped and fallen twice in the last week, I stayed home from school.
    Fordi der var is på vejen, og jeg allerede var skredet og faldet to gange i den sidste uge, blev jeg hjemme fra skole.

  2. My sister, although she showed more resilience than I had ever seen her have before, still finished the marathon second.
    Min søster, selvom hun viste mere modstandsdygtighed, end jeg nogensinde havde set hende have før, afsluttede stadig maraton nummer to.

They can also be quite short:

  1. I took out the trash because it stunk.
    Jeg tog skraldet ud, fordi det stinkede.

  2. It was dark in the hallway, so Marta illuminated it after she found a book of matches in her backpack.
    Det var mørkt på gangen, så Marta oplyste den, efter hun havde fundet en tændstikbog i sin rygsæk.

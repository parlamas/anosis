1. hel (adj. whole, entire)

    1. Jeg har en hel liste.
      I have a whole list
    2. Vælg hele tabellen.
      Select the entire table.
    3. Det er en hel liste.
      It's quite a list.
    4. Der er en hel række ting.
      There are a whole range of things.

  2. hele et (noun entity → whole)

    1. Projectet skal vurderes som et hele.
      The project must be assessed as a whole.
    2. Det var såmænd det hele
      That was really all of it
    3. Det hele passer sammen.
      It all fits together.

  3. heller (adv. either)

    1. We are not interested either.
      Vi er heller ikke interesserede.
    2. Rådet heller ikke.
      Neither does the council.
    3. They did not know it, and neither did I.
      De vidste det ikke, og det vidste jeg heller ikke.

  4. hellere (adv. rather(vil)/had better(må))

    1. Du må hellere sove i nærheden.
      You'd better sleep nearby.
    2. There is no place I would rather be.
      Der er ikke noget sted, jeg hellere vil være.
    3. I'd rather leave now.
      Jeg vil hellere gå nu.
    4. I'd better leave now.
      Jeg må hellere tage afsted nu.

  5. helst (adv. preferably/rather/ideally)

    1. Hun bad om en anden læge, helst en kvinde.
      She asked for another doctor, preferably a woman.
    2. De ville helst komme forbi i næste uge.
      They would rather come by next week.
    3. Hun ville helst arbejde hjemme.
      Ideally, she would like to work at home.

  6. helt (adv. quite/completely/entirely/totally/fully/truly)

    1. Han havde helt glemt, at det var mors dag.
      He had completely forgotten that it was mother's day.
    2. Hans version af hændelserne er ikke helt sandfærdig.
      His version of the events is not entirely true.
    3. Jeg er helt forvirret.
      I'm totally confused.

  7. hverken ... eller ... (adv. ... neither ... nor ...)

    1. Hun hverken danser eller synger.
      She neither danses nor sings.
    2. Jeg har ikke set hverken ham eller hans kone.
      I have not seen either him or his wife.

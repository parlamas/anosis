Lydfil nummer nioghalvtreds: Skolegang

Tom har en dreng i sjette klasse, og han har lige været til forældermøde henne på skolen. Her snakkede de om, hvordan det går i klassen, og de snakkede også om hjemmearbejde.

Nogle af forældrene synes, at børnene har for meget hjemmearbejder. De synes, at børn skal have fri, når de kommer hjem fra skole. Men Tom synes ikke, at børnene har for meget hjemmearbejde. Faktisk synes han, at de har for lidt, og han vil gerne have, at de lærer noget mere — men måske er jeg bare lidt gammeldags, tænker Tom. Han er ældre, end de andre forældre. Han er 59, og de fleste andre er i fyrrene.

Tom sidder lidt og tænker tilbage på sin egen skoletid. Han begyndte i folkeskolen, da han var syv år. Dengang var der meget disciplin i skolen. Børnene sagde 'De' til lærnene, og de skulle rejse sig op, når læreren kom ind i klassen. De måtte først sætte sig, når læreren sagde værsgo*. Nogle af lærerne var meget skrappe — især matematiklæreren. Ham var Tom lidt bange for. Han gav lussinger, hvis børnene var frække, eller hvis de snakkede i timerne. Men er var også andre straffe. Man kunne f.eks. få en eftersidning, hvis man kom for sent, eller hvis man ikke havde lavet hjemmearbejde.

Sådan er det ikke i dag, og det er godt, synes Tom. I dag siger børnene 'du' og fornavn til lærerne, og der er en tryg atmosfære i klassen. Undervisningen i dag er også anderledes end dengang. I Toms skoletid skulle børnene lære mange ting udenad. Såden arbejder man ikke i dag. I dag skal børnene lære at arbejde selvstændigt. De skal også arbejde i grupper, fordi de skal lære at samarbejde.

Tom synes, at det er godt, at børnene føler sig trygge, og at de ikke er bange for læarerne, men han synes, at der er for meget larm i timerne. Han synes ikke, der er nok disciplin, og han synes ikke, at børnene har nok respekt for lærerne.

Audio file number fifty-nine: Schooling

Tom has a boy in sixth grade, and he just went to a parent-teacher conference at school. Here they talked about how things are going in class and they also talked about homework.

Some of the parents think that the children have too much homework. They think that children should have time off when they get home from school. But Tom doesn't think the children have too much homework. In fact, he thinks they have too little and he wants them to learn something more — but maybe I'm just a little old-fashioned, Tom thinks. He is older than the other parents. He is 59 and most others are in their forties.

Tom sits for a while and thinks back to his own school days. He started primary school when he was seven years old. Back then there was a lot of discipline in the school. The children said 'De' to the teachers and they had to stand up when the teacher entered the class. They were only allowed to sit down when the teacher said "Sit down!" Some of the teachers were very strict — especially the math teacher. Tom was a little afraid of him. He slapped the children if they were naughty or if they talked during class. But there were also other punishments. One could e.g. get a late sitting if you were late or if you hadn't done your homework.

It's not like that today, and that's good, Tom thinks. Today, children say 'you' and their first name to the teachers and there is a safe atmosphere in the class. Teaching today is also different from then. During Tom's school days, the children had to learn many things by heart. You don't work like that today. Today, children must learn to work independently. They also have to work in groups because they have to learn to cooperate.

Tom thinks that it is good that children feel safe and that they are not afraid of the teachers, but he thinks that there is too much noise in class. He doesn't think there is enough discipline and he doesn't think children have enough respect for the teachers.
